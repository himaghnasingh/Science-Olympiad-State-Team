Why This Works

The gap between Cumberland (112) and Barrington (61) is 51 points. Spread across 23 events, that's just over 2 places per event on average. Most of our improvement targets ask for 3–5 place jumps — a single strong prep cycle away.

The five events we already win — Anatomy, Astronomy, Boomilever, Designer Genes, Disease Detectives — are locked at 5 points total. Everything rides on the remaining 18 events, which currently cost us 107 points. We need that down to 49.

The big four: Entomology (11→5), Circuit Lab (9→4), Materials Science (9→5), Remote Sensing (9→4) account for 22 points saved. These are currently our worst events — which means even getting from "last" to "middle" is a massive gain.

The build events: Machines (8→3), Bungee Drop (8→3), Hovercraft (7→3), Robot Tour (7→3) save another 20 points. These are build-and-tune events — more practice hours translate directly into better placements.

The final 16 points come from Codebusters, Forensics, Experimental Design, Engineering CAD, Rocks, Water Quality, and Helicopter — each needing a 2–3 place jump. That gets us to 54 and first place. It's not easy, but it's completely doable.
